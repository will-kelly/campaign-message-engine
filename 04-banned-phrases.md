# Banned phrases

The engine will not use any of the words, phrases, or patterns below in any channel — unless your voice guide explicitly unlocks one. Upload this file to the project knowledge base.

The list isn't arbitrary. Each banned item collapses signal into noise. They're banned because they make every brand sound like every other brand.

---

## Hype words

These were once load-bearing. They've been hollowed out by overuse. They now signal that the writer ran out of specific things to say.

- Revolutionary
- Game-changer / game-changing
- Cutting-edge
- Next-generation / next-gen
- World-class
- Best-in-class
- Industry-leading
- Innovative (used as a self-descriptor)
- Disruptive (in a marketing context — fine in business strategy contexts)
- Transformative
- Groundbreaking
- Robust (when used as a generic intensifier — "robust solution")
- Seamless / seamlessly
- Effortless / effortlessly
- Unparalleled
- Unprecedented (overused since 2020)

---

## Hollow verbs

These verbs feel active but communicate nothing specific.

- Leverage (use "use")
- Unlock (use the actual benefit — "save $X" or "ship in Y days")
- Synergize / drive synergies
- Supercharge
- Empower (specifically: "empower teams to" — usually means nothing)
- Streamline (only acceptable when paired with a specific process)
- Elevate
- Amplify (when used vaguely)
- Optimize (only acceptable when paired with a specific metric)
- Maximize (same — needs a specific metric)
- Curate / curated (overused outside its original meaning)

---

## Empty filler phrases

These are throat-clearing. They delay the point.

- "In today's [adjective] world" / "In today's fast-paced world" / "In today's digital landscape"
- "Now more than ever"
- "We're excited to announce" (use a real verb: "introducing," "launching," or just state the news)
- "We're thrilled to share"
- "It's that simple" (it usually isn't)
- "At the end of the day"
- "Move the needle"
- "Take it to the next level"
- "Stay tuned"
- "Watch this space"
- "Without further ado"
- "Long story short"
- "To make a long story short"
- "Needless to say" (if it's needless, don't say it)
- "It goes without saying" (then don't say it)

## LinkedIn slop patterns

These openers tell the reader the post is generic before they finish reading the first line.

- "Hot take:"
- "Real talk:"
- "Unpopular opinion:" (usually followed by a popular opinion)
- "Here's the thing:"
- "Plot twist:"
- "I'll be honest" (be honest by default)
- "I'm going to be transparent here"
- "Let that sink in"
- "Read that again"
- "If you do nothing else today, do this:"
- Hook → "👇" → list
- "Most people don't realize..."
- "Nobody talks about..." (usually a topic many people are talking about)
- "Here's what nobody tells you about..."

---

## Cliché constructions

Sentence patterns that have been repeated until they mean nothing.

- "Not just X, but Y" (overused — fine occasionally, banned as a default move)
- "X is more than just Y"
- "It's not about X. It's about Y."
- "The future of [industry] is [vague claim]"
- "We're not just a [category]. We're a [category with adjective]."
- "X is the new Y"
- "X without Y" (only acceptable when literally true and specific)
- "From X to Y in Z" (only acceptable with specific numbers)

## AI tells

Phrases that signal an LLM wrote the copy without enough specific instruction. These trigger reader skepticism instantly.

- "Let me explain"
- "Let's dive in"
- "Let's dive deeper"
- "In essence"
- "In conclusion" (in copy that doesn't need a conclusion)
- "It's worth noting that"
- "It's important to understand that"
- "When it comes to..."
- "In the world of..."
- "In the realm of..."
- "Navigate the complexities of"
- "Embark on a journey"
- "A wealth of"
- "A myriad of"
- "Plethora"
- "Delve into"
- "Tapestry"
- "Landscape" (used as a generic noun: "the marketing landscape," "the technology landscape")
- "Ecosystem" (when used vaguely)

---

## Engagement bait

Closers that try to manipulate the algorithm rather than earn a response.

- "Drop a 🔥 if you agree"
- "Tag someone who needs to hear this"
- "Comment YES if..."
- "Like if you've ever..."
- "Share if you found this valuable"
- "Save this post for later" (as a closer)
- "Follow for more content like this"
- "What did I miss?" (only acceptable when the post genuinely invites correction)
- "Thoughts?" (when the post hasn't earned a response)
- "Agree or disagree?"

---

## Generic CTAs

These hide what the reader will actually get. Replace them with specific phrases that describe the actual action.

- "Learn more" → "See how it works" or "Read the breakdown"
- "Click here" → never. Hyperlink a descriptive phrase.
- "Get started" → "Start a free trial" or "Book a 20-min demo"
- "Sign up today" → name the thing they're signing up for
- "Contact us" → "Book a call" or "Send us a 2-line brief"
- "Find out more" → describe what they'll find
- "Discover" + noun ("Discover our platform") — describe what they'll see instead

---

## Em dash misuse

The em dash is fine when grammatically right — interrupting a clause, setting off an aside, marking a strong break. It's banned as filler when a comma, period, or colon would do the same job.

Bad: "We help companies scale — and grow — faster."
Bad: "Our platform — which is robust — handles everything."

Acceptable: "We shrink onboarding to 9 days — without engineering work."
Acceptable: "The fix isn't tooling. It's data ownership — and most teams can't see it."

---

## Three-item lists where two would do

Marketing copy reflexively triples up nouns and verbs to sound thorough. Two specific items beats three vague items every time.

Bad: "We help you plan, execute, and optimize your campaigns."
Bad: "Faster, smarter, better content."
Bad: "Strategy, execution, and results."

Better: "We help you ship campaigns and learn from them."
Better: "Faster content. Better arguments."
Better: "Strategy that survives execution."

## When a banned phrase is appropriate

The engine will check whether a banned phrase appears in your voice guide as explicitly unlocked. If it does, the ban is lifted for that phrase. If not, the engine paraphrases or restructures.

To unlock a banned phrase, add a section to your voice guide titled "Unlocked vocabulary" and list the phrases you use deliberately. Example:

> Unlocked vocabulary:
> - "leverage" (we use this when describing infrastructure use)
> - "platform" (industry term, can't avoid it)

Without an unlock, every banned phrase will be replaced.
